About

I am an interdisciplinary AI-accounting researcher and educator. My research runs from the technical foundations of large language models, including their internal representations, interpretability, and reliability, to their use on financial information, alongside empirical work in asset pricing, accounting quality, and corporate disclosure. My recent work applies large language models to core accounting problems and has appeared in Decision Support Systems (ABDC A*) and the Journal of Computer Information Systems (ABDC A).

I hold a PhD in Accounting from Victoria University of Wellington (2010) and an MS in Applied Data Science from the University of Chicago (2025). I have taught accounting, finance, and business analytics for almost fifteen years at five institutions in three countries. Between 2018 and 2023 I worked in industry as a fintech entrepreneur and consultant, where I co-created an online financial statement analysis course that reached more than three million learners and developed production AI/ML systems.

Teaching

Preparing future leaders to think critically in the age of AI.

Courses taught

Across undergraduate and MBA levels: financial, managerial, intermediate, and cost accounting; financial statement analysis; corporate finance and financial management; quantitative methods and business analytics; and information technology management.

In the classroom

I build AI into the learning experience to simulate how work is now done in the modern workplace. In my Information Technology Management course, for example, student teams design and build working mobile applications with AI tools, from AI-assisted wireframing through development in Google AI Studio, managing the three-week project with Scrum. In my MBA analytics course, students learn to use AI to generate Python code and conduct complex data analyses.
